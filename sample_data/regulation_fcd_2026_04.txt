FINANCIAL CONDUCT DIRECTIVE 2026/04
Customer Data Protection and Operational Resilience Requirements

1. Scope and Purpose
1.1 This directive applies to all licensed financial institutions operating within the
jurisdiction. It establishes minimum requirements for customer data protection,
record keeping, incident management and third-party oversight.

2. Record Keeping
2.1 Institutions must retain all customer transaction records for a minimum period of
seven years from the date of the transaction.
2.2 Records must be stored in a tamper-evident format and must remain retrievable
within five business days of a regulatory request.

3. Customer Data Protection
3.1 Institutions must encrypt all personal data of customers both at rest and in
transit using industry-standard encryption algorithms.
3.2 Institutions shall obtain explicit consent from data subjects before processing
personal data for any purpose beyond the original service agreement.
3.3 Access to customer personal data must be restricted to authorized personnel on a
need-to-know basis, and all access must be logged.

4. Incident Reporting
4.1 Institutions must notify the regulator of any data breach affecting customer
information within seventy-two hours of discovery.
4.2 A root cause analysis report shall be submitted to the regulator within thirty
days of any reportable incident.

5. Third-Party Oversight
5.1 Institutions are required to perform due diligence on all third-party service
providers that process customer data before engagement and at least annually
thereafter.
5.2 Outsourcing agreements must include audit rights enabling the institution and the
regulator to inspect the service provider's controls.

6. Governance and Training
6.1 Senior management shall review the institution's data protection posture at least
quarterly and document the outcomes of each review.
6.2 All employees must complete data protection training within thirty days of
joining and annually thereafter.
